# Skill Candidates — 2026-07-20

What your own AI conversation history says is worth encoding into reusable
skills. Found by scanning 214 conversations across Claude Cowork and Claude

## 1. studio-proposal-drafting
**What it covers:** Turning discovery-call notes into a client proposal —
scope summary, three package options, timeline, and your standard terms.
**Seen in your history:** 11 matching requests across 9 sessions,
February to July. For example: "turn these call notes into a proposal like
the Hartmann one, three options again" · "same proposal structure as last
time, this client is a bakery chain".
**Why it's worth encoding:** You rebuilt the same proposal structure at
least 11 times, and in 6 of those you pasted a previous proposal in first
to remind the tool of your format. The format already exists — it lives in
your pasting habit instead of in a skill.

## 2. studio-shoot-day-runsheet
**What it covers:** Building the run sheet for a brand-shoot day: shot
list from the moodboard, call times, location notes, and the packing
checklist.
**Seen in your history:** 7 matching requests across 7 sessions, March to
July — one per shoot. For example: "make the run sheet for Thursday's
shoot, moodboard attached" · "shoot day plan again — two locations this
time".
**Why it's worth encoding:** Every shoot got its own hand-built run sheet,
and three of the seven requests corrected the same forgotten item (the
packing list) after the fact. A skill would carry the checklist so you
stop re-remembering it.

## 3. finance-month-end-invoicing
**What it covers:** The month-end pass: which projects hit an invoicing
milestone, drafting the invoice lines, and the reminder email for anything
unpaid past 14 days.
**Seen in your history:** 6 matching requests across 6 sessions, one at
each month end since February. For example: "which projects do I invoice
this month, here's the tracker export" · "draft the reminder for the two
unpaid ones, friendly tone".
**Why it's worth encoding:** The same three-part routine ran at every
month end in your history, each time assembled from scratch across two or
three requests. Encoded once, it becomes one command on the 1st.

## 4. content-case-study-writeup
**What it covers:** Turning a finished project into a website case study —
before/after framing, three process photos picked from the delivery
folder, client quote request email.
**Seen in your history:** at least 5 matching requests across 4 sessions,
April to June — likely more under different wording, since your case-study
asks phrase the same request several ways. For example: "write up the
Reinhart rebrand as a case study like the others" · "case study for the
packaging project — same structure".
**Why it's worth encoding:** "Like the others" appears in four of the five
requests — the structure is stable in your head and re-explained to the
tool every time.

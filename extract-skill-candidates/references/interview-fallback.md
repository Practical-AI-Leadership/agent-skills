# Interview Fallback

The path when there is no usable history: the scan found nothing (or only a
handful of conversations), the user declined the install, or the index
turned out too thin to ground candidates. Also the top-up when the scan
covered only part of the user's work — say which part came from where.

Open by saying plainly why the interview is happening ("your history is too
thin to read routines out of, so let's find them by talking instead").

## The questions

Ask in plain language, a few at a time — a conversation, not a form. Adapt
wording to what is already known about the person; skip questions they have
already answered in passing.

1. **The week's regulars.** "What do you find yourself doing every week —
   or several times a week — that follows roughly the same steps each
   time?"
2. **The multi-step chores.** "Which recurring task has the most steps —
   the one where you have to remember what comes after what?"
3. **The re-explained.** "What do you keep re-explaining — to an AI tool, a
   colleague, or a new hire — because it lives only in your head?"
4. **The reused prompts.** "Do you keep prompts, templates, or checklists
   somewhere that you paste in again and again? Which ones?"
5. **The produced-on-cadence.** "What do you produce on a schedule —
   reports, updates, invoices, posts, reviews?"
6. **The dreaded one.** "Which recurring task do you push off the longest?
   Dread usually marks the ones most worth encoding."

Follow up on each promising answer with the two facts a candidate needs:
**how often** it happens, and **what the steps roughly are** — enough to
see whether it has a repeatable shape, not a full procedure.

## Turning answers into candidates

Apply the same candidate lens and quality bar as the scan path
(`shortlist-format.md`): it comes back, it has a repeatable shape, encoding
it would return real time. Drop one-offs and umbrellas exactly as there.

The evidence block differs: interview-derived candidates carry **"From our
conversation"** with what the user reported ("you said this runs every
Friday and takes about an hour"), never dressed up as scan evidence. If the
user was vague about frequency, keep the vagueness visible ("a few times a
month, by your estimate").

The report and chat summary follow `shortlist-format.md` unchanged, with
one added line up front: candidates here come from what the user told me,
not from scanned history — worth re-checking against reality after a few
weeks of use.
